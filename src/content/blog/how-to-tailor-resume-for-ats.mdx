# How to Tailor Your Resume for ATS in 2026

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) filter the majority of resumes before a human ever sees them. Learning how to tailor your resume for ATS is essential for getting shortlisted in 2026.

## What is ATS?

ATS software scans your resume for keywords, structure, and formatting. Resumes that don’t match the job description or that use complex layouts often get rejected before recruiters see them. Tailoring your resume for ATS means aligning your content and format with what these systems (and the job description) expect.

## Why Tailoring for ATS Matters

- **Keyword match**: ATS ranks candidates by how well the resume matches the job description. Missing key terms can drop your score.
- **Structure**: Simple, clear headings (Experience, Education, Skills) parse correctly. Tables and graphics often don’t.
- **Consistency**: Job titles and skills that mirror the JD improve your chances. Generic resumes underperform.

## How to Tailor Your Resume for ATS

1. **Use the job description as your guide**  
   Pull out required skills, tools, and qualifications. Use the same or very similar wording where it’s accurate for your experience.

2. **Lead with impact**  
   Start bullets with action verbs and add metrics (percentages, numbers, scale). This helps both ATS and recruiters.

3. **Keep formatting simple**  
   Use standard section names, bullet points, and a single-column layout. Avoid images, headers/footers, and complex tables.

4. **Include a skills section**  
   List technical and soft skills that appear in the JD. Place the most relevant ones near the top.

5. **Check your ATS score**  
   Use a free [ATS score checker](/tools/ats-score-checker) to see how well your resume matches a job description before you apply.
